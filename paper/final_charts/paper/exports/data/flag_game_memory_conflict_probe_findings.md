# Memory Conflict Probe Findings

- GPT-5.4 produces private-compatible third-country answers under weak compatible evidence: 22.2% at m=1 and 24.1% at m=3; GPT-4o is 0.0% and 0.0%.
- GPT-5.4 also makes many private-compatible third-country answers when social evidence contradicts weak private evidence: 46.3% at m=1 and 38.9% at m=3.
- Reasoning entries (m=3) mainly reduce direct social copying rather than expanding off-axis private-compatible answers: GPT-5.4 direct social-evidence choices fall from 15.7% to 11.1%; GPT-4o falls from 21.9% to 17.3%.
- The incompatible other band is small but concentrated in GPT-5.4 strong-private contradictory trials: 7.4% at m=1 and 6.5% at m=3. These are mostly visually plausible but wrong color/stripe confusions.

Draft caption: In a single-agent memory-conflict probe, GPT-4o behaves largely as a binary target-versus-social selector. GPT-5.4 more often chooses off-axis countries that remain compatible with the private image. Adding reasons (m=3) makes both models less likely to directly copy social evidence; for GPT-5.4, the main effect is stronger private-evidence retention rather than a larger off-axis band.
